**Chris Bache: ** I have a lot of energy around that question, right? Because it has very far-reaching implications from everything from think tank phenomena and dynamics for augmenting creativity, for enhancing creativity, all the way to understanding a new context for understanding some of the dynamics of social and cultural evolution and psycho-spiritual evolution. So I think, for example, I think of an individual, a person, as, from a reincarnational perspective, as a living walking group, all right? And so the more lifetimes a person has experienced, the more likely it is that they're going to have kind of more intelligence, more to show, more empathy, more understanding, more skills of various things. But in a sense, they are a living community of themselves. When you have a group of people come together, if you're coming together either for spiritual purposes or for social discussion, or for research purposes, or think tank purposes, what this suggests is, if you can harmonize yourselves psychologically, socially, and even physiologically, you can augment, you can stimulate and augment your own functioning by augmenting a group consciousness, a group mind, which can have an uplifting and animating and creativity-enhancing effect on the individual minds of those present. And this, I think, has significant implications for how we go about learning at a more pedestrian level, and how we go about creating new knowledge at a collective level. It also has profound ramifications for understanding some of the social dynamics involved. If we have a classroom context, and this would generalize out to the culture at large, which is highly divisive, then we don't have this kind of intelligence-enhancing, empathy-enhancing effect of a unified field, drawing us into a higher state of sensitivity, a higher state of knowing. If we do have a congealed group, if we have a good social process for congealing a group, then we actually stimulate, I believe, a more potent form of knowing, a more potent kind of discovery, because the group mind is like a more powerful transceiver that connects the individuals to the divine mind, so to speak. Even to the point where we are clearly at a certain evolutionary impasse. We need to break through certain barriers, in order to solve certain major technological questions that we have come to in physics, in the sciences, which are really important for environmental purposes, cold fusion, number of important unified field theories. I think we're looking at here, at a planet which, if the planet continues to be fragmented, politically, racially, gender, even fragmented in terms of speciesism, then that condition of collective fragmentation is preventing a potentiation of our individual genius. Where on the alternative, if we move towards greater integration, greater social cohesion, greater inclusiveness in medical care, for example, towards political democracy, so on and so forth, then that would have the cumulative effect of potentially literally potentiating the performance of our individual minds and therefore making it easier for us to have the breakthroughs that would have profound implications in technology, as well as the humanities and the arts. But I'm thinking particularly of the sciences, where we have a sense that we're really up against some real major barriers for solving some of the technologies that we need to solve to really get a grip on some of the environmental issues we're dealing with. So I think to understand the non-local quality of mind has profound social implications. Once we begin to understand that how our mind functions, not only how comfortable it is with itself, but how creative it is, how imaginative it is, is not simply a function of the individual health, the health of my individual mind, but is a function of the health of our collective mind. Then we have a vested interest in the health of our collective mind, and we have a vested interest in the health of a healthy society, in a healthy culture, in a healthy globe. To serve that end cannot but have a rebounding effect, an uplifting effect on our individual well-being directly, not indirectly, and not by virtue of virtue, but directly because of morphic resonance, because of the nature of mind itself.
